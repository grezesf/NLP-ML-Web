RESISTOR 10K 10KILOOHMS

FILM CAPACITOR 100nf code 104

The answer to your question depends entirely upon the circuit in which the component will be used. However, using a 9V battery as the only power source limits what damage you can do. :)

For instance, it's not unusual (and this is from old, old memory, so I could be wrong) for an overdrive or compression pedal to be capable of amplifying a signal times 100. However, you'd wear a 9 volt battery out pretty fast if it was consistently used at that setting...

As long as all your components are rated to higher than the voltage and current you will be using, you will be fine.

In your example, a 250v capacitor will easily handle whatever you throw at it from a 9v supply.

First of all, do you have a schematic along with your parts list there? That will be important.

I recommend you visit the forums on Guitar PCB and read through their newbie section. They have a crash course PDF that will help answer some of these questions: http://www.guitarpcb.com/apps/forums/topics/show/7156859-crash-course-in-pedal-building-

A 250V capacitor is likely way too much for within a pedal. It will be physically larger. Anything over 12V should be fine, but I'd probably get 25V or 35V audio grade caps. Resistors can all be 1/4 Watt as you've mentioned.

Mammoth electronics sells lots of parts for DIY pedal builders.

There is also a good step-by-step of someone building a pedal, along with his parts search recommendations: http://www.instructables.com/id/DIY-Rat-Clone-Distortion-Guitar-Effect-Pedal---The/
