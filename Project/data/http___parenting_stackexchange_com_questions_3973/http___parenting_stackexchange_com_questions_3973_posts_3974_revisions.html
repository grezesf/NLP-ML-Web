Firstly, in the event of the body needing energy for stuff like digestion or growing (pretty much 99% of a baby's daily activities), the body generally diverts blood away from the limbs, which are basically useless to a newborn, to towards the core "core", where the real work's being done by the heart and stomach, and to the brain.

This is especially common in newborns, which as their limbs are basically useless until they're mobile, while pretty much 99% of their daily activities are growing and digesting. At the same time, their brain is doing an awful lot of growing and "re-wiring". The general recommendation I've found is to check the heat of the back of the neck (path from heart to brain) in preparation for useful tasks, such as getting your attention and the torso (path from heart to everything else) being cute on demand.

The general recommendation I've found is to check the heat of the back of the neck (path from heart to brain) and heat of the torso (contains everything else).

Also, be aware that any uncovered part of the body will get cold faster than any covered part, especially the "sticky-out" bits (hands, feet, ears, etc.), because that's where the heat can get out from.
