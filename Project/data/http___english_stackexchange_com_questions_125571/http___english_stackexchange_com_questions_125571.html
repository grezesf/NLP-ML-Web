Enquiry forms on websites [closed]

up vote -2 down vote favorite

My university has registered with an online e-book website. But, after downloading an e-book, I was asked for a password. The enquiry that I wrote is:

I want to know where can I find passwords for e-books that I have downloaded.

Is this statement polite? How can it be re-framed so as to be more coherent?

As Brad has said, want is at best brusque. At worst it's the language of a childish tantrum, so it's generally best avoided in more formal situations. A more usual way of asking is (my emphasis to show the difference):

I would like to know where I can find passwords for the e-books I have downloaded.

It's not quite impolite, but people tend to find want a bit brusque. (The same is true in German.) It's generally more polite to soften requests with may or could: "May I please ask where I can find...?"
