Background:

I'm surprised that so many questions have fewer votes than answers. I ask myself "Self, why wouldn't the Answerer upvote the question, L'Chatchilah? Especially since the Answerer is implicitly prompting the Questioner and others to upvote the answer and grant reputation, why not show some goodwill?"

(BTW, if this bugs you too, why not be chumradik (stringent) and make sure you vote on the questions you answered?)

Question:

If a question provokes your answer, it's at least DerechDerekh Eretz (good manners) and Hakarat HaTov (gratitude) to vote on the original question. Does any halachic reason obligate you to upvote (or downvote) the question you answer?
