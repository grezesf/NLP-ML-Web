Is yogurt affected by human emotions?

up vote 0 down vote favorite

The documentary I Am (Wikipedia) makes a number of claims, including that more signals are sent from the heart to the brain than vice-versa, and that the your emotion or mood can have an effect on objects around you.

In particular, a test is shown measuring signals sent out by yogurt, and showing it is affected by emotions.

This seems far-fetched. Does this test demonstrate yogurt is directly affected by the emotions of people around it?

There are some people here who claim that they haven't been able to reproduce the experiment's results.

One comment says,

Thank you for the reply. I did contact the Institute of Heart Math. While Rollin McCraty himself was not available, his assistance Jackie was very helpful. This is what she told me: "What we used was a voltmeter but you need to find a very sensitive one that measures in the millivolt and microvolt ranges. You also need to use plain yogurt, just under room temperature and organic worked the best. (don’t use the non-fat or low-fat… just regular plain). Use a plastic dish or glass but not metal and do the readings in a quiet room where all of the electronic gadgets are off or even unplugged. All cellphones, ipods, etc need to be totally off or in another location. Watches should also be removed as well as earrings and other metal that the subject could be wearing."

Now we couldn't recreate the experiment, but I think that is because we were using a digital voltmeter, rather than an analog voltmeter as is used in the film. Thus, while we had no electronic gadgets in the room recommended), our measuring device itself was electronic.

If you want to try this yourself, go analog.

A user called "I AM Team" says,

Hey…. We can guarantee that no tricks we played in this experiment, no slight of hand, under the table shenanigans! But as Tom expressed we did relax protocol to film… Sorry for any trouble this may cause you. Peace

Without knowing what they did (what "protocols were relaxed"), it's difficult to comment on whether and what "truth" can be determined from the experimental result.

I don't know whether it's relevant (and I certainly can't prove that it is), but FYI elsewhere on this site is someone being skeptical about the way in which people use meters in their experiments.
