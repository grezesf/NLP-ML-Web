Have phenomenologists considered poetic subjectivities in their investigation of things-for-us?

up vote 0 down vote favorite

Dylan Thomas the late Welsh poet said:

“A good poem helps to change the shape and significance of the universe, helps to extend everyone's knowledge of himself and the world around him.”

This makes it appear that Husserls Phenomenology should have some overlap with the idea of poetic subjectivity; where he considers things-for-us that is perhaps the universe-for-us.

Is this the case? Do (or have) phenomenologists consider poetic subjectivities?
