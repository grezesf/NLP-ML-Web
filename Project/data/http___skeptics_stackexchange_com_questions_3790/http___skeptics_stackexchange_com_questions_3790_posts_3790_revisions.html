(This question was prompted by the discussion at http://skeptics.stackexchange.com/questions/3765/ )

The blog post that started this claims that since the earths energy consumption increases by 2% every year, energy consumption will double every 35 years, and at some point in the future humanity will generate so much energy that it would be equivalent to earth orbiting not once, but two suns.

Although the maths can't be faulted, a 2% increase per year will indeed mean you double around every 35-36 years, and a continued doubling every 36 years would indeed mean we increased our output 8000 fold in some 450 years, making it roughly in the same vicinity as what we get from the sun.

But the blog post doesn't talk about mathematics, but claims that this will happen unless we stop using all forms of energy that is are not solar-driven, ie i.e. both fossil and nuclear fuel.

Question: Is there any reason to believe humanity will continue to increase it's its energy use consumption by 2% for the next 500 years even if we continue to use fossil and nuclear energy?
