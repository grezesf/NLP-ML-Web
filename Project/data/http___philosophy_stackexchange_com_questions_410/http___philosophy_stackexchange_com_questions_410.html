The more axiological material (value) is externalized (deontology, utility, consequence) the more it tends toward assuming the role of ontology (fact): ie, the facticities (alleged facts) are measured/measurable and by some scale, presentable as comparable (no hidden weights, no inhering values). The more axiological material is internalized (connaturalisms, religious conviction(?), naive conscience) the more it tends toward mystification... or the problem of induction/abduction and the mechanism/s behind the intuition's proper weights on facts (value as discerned but indiscernible how by other minds).

It seems to me utilitarianism, very broadly speaking, attempts to reduce the axiological material to a quantitative rather than qualitative state, ie, to take value-embued things to some reduced number of enumerable variables (legal view) rather than a qualitative complex of vital unity (eg Bergson: elan vitale, dynamic religion).

Question reframed: can any qualitative material be reformulated so that it appears quantitative? Answer: yes, when it's finite enough and someone successfully does it, and, no, until someone does it. Consequence: all ontology seems a neutral collection of facts which may be viewed axiologically or may BE axiological and these axiological features may and often are reduced so that they disappear into ontology itself (happens all the time in economy). This, however, doesn't mean that ontology is a meta-axiology of the axiological. For, where ontology does not yet cover some bit of emotion, value, irrational tendency, apparent teleology, ... it has, thereby, failed to explain it de facto, nor is it a guaranteed apparatus (meta-ethic) - so, absolutely not - NOT ALL SYSTEMS OF ETHICS CAN BE RECONSTRUCTED IN UTILITARIAN TERMS without doing violence to the scope, intent, content, frame, or something whereby "value" in its utilitarian mask cannot not translate to the same "value" in the original formulated ethic.

(If you read John Venn's book Theory of Probability, he addresses this duality of interpretation in statistics and finds it indissolvable as well, which is frighteningly instructive! Thus statistics is always ad hoc and always metaphysical in its essence, or how we interpret such. C.S. Pierce concludes this by demonstrating the insufficiencies of deductive and inductive methods of reasoning, since they both reify the parameters in order to ensure the results, whereas abduction is perfectly naive and probably not reducible but possibly more accurate! Ethics then could be seen as indissolvable as Probability, since the health of both tend to depend on a versatile perspectivism about the nature of freedom and determinism. Nietzsche is most instructive here.)

The problem with this approach is that it fails to distinguish between two completely different types of consequences. First, there are consequences that involve intrinsic goods, such as telling the truth and not killing. Second, there are more indirect consequences in which we might say that the ends justify the means – such as stealing a loaf of bread to feed one’s starving family. True morality should focus on intrinsic goods, without attempting to counterbalance these against the second and more indirect type of consequences.

At the close of World War II, President Harry Truman used consequentialist reasoning when deciding to drop atomic bombs on the Japanese civilians. On one side of the balance, Truman placed the negative consequence of killing tens of thousands of innocent Japanese civilians. On the other side of the balance, Truman considered that the bombs would bring a quick end to the war, and thus serve as a useful means to an end. Believing that the latter reasoning was more weighty, he decided to drop the bombs – a murderous decision.

Thus, consequentialism is not only flawed but even hazardous when applied to decisions like this.

But first let see what utilitarianism is all about? From Microsoft Encarta Dictionary we have:

Thus utilitarianism is really a fuzzy ethical system. If what you do makes people happy by 20 points, while what I do make'em happy by 50 points, then my action is better than yours. It's that simple.
