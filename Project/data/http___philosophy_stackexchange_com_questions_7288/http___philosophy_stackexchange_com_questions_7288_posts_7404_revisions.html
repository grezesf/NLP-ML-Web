Is the idea that a universe can create itself nonsense?

To act or otherwise have an effect, one must in some sense exist. Creation is an action, therefore in order to create one must first exist, and be in existence, at the time of creation. At least the creator must be in existence at the time that the chain of events leading to creation was initiated. It is impossible for one to act or have an effect if one does not exist.

To be created is to come into existence. To transition from not-being to being. Yet it would be incorrect to say that one can be in a state of not-being, as if one existed and simply had the quality of not-existing. That would be illogical as it would be defining something as both one thing and the direct contradiction of that thing.

The question pertains to the universe, but what exactly is that? The universe is space and time. Space being filled with physical matter and the forces and laws that govern that matter. One might also call this the physical or natural universe.

Space and time are intimately connected. Matter does not exist outside of time, and time does not exist without matter. Since, in the creation of the universe we have an event in which both of these things are being created simultaneously we cannot say that the universe is created at any other point than the beginning of time. Whatever created the universe, did so outside of time, prior to the beginning of time (though here "prior" must have an unusual meaning). But we know that the universe consists of time and space, and where the universe is there is time and space. So the universe cannot both be time and space and create itself before time, as it would not be itself at that time, it would be something else.

To answer briefly the question of whether the universe can create itself: no, time forbids it in two senses. Firstly, one cannot act in the past. Secondly, the universe cannot be and not be at the same time.  

The subsequent question of what created the universe, can be pointed to on the basis of the answer so far: Something outside of space and time. Judging by the scale and intricacy of the observable universe it was something very powerful and precise. Looking at humanity and our reason, our achievements in art and science, our capacity for emotion and so on, it was something that gave birth to unlimited potential. Given the unlikeliness of human potential flourishing I would say that whatever created the universe that we live in did so purposefully to enable human flourishing.

But the question is whether it is nonsense to say the universe created itself. Well, strictly, yes, for the reasons summarized here: to create one must exist, to be created one begins to exist when one did not previously exist, a thing cannot be and not be simultaneously.
