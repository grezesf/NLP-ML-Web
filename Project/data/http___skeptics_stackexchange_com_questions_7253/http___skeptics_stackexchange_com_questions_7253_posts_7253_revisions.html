Most of us will have been taught the two second-second rule when learning to drive.

The two-second rule is a rule of thumb by rule of thumb by which a driver may maintain a safe following distance at any speed. The rule is that a driver should ideally stay at least two seconds behind any vehicle that is directly in front of the driver's vehicle.

But, what research lends is there to lend weight to this rule of thumb?

I always liked to think that giving a 2 second gap between you and the vehicle in front was clearly a sensible idea. Half a second to notice, half a second to react, half a second to compensate for the differences between vehicles and half a second for safety all seemed like good ballpark figures.
