Why did Socrates consider the donkey to be a perverse animal?

up vote 0 down vote favorite

In the Phaedo, Plato has Socrates saying (and Cebes agreeing with him) that:

Well, those who have cultivated gluttony or selfishness or drunkenness, instead of taking pains to avoid them, are likely to assume the form of donkeys and other perverse animals. Don't you think so?

Yes, that is very likely.

Why did they consider the donkey to be a perverse animal? Was this a common belief amongst ancient Greeks or something idiosyncratic to Plato or those he wrote about?
